## 脱口秀段落结构模板库

Version: v0.1

# 1. Purpose


A bit is a complete comedy unit.

Structure:

Idea
 ↓
Story
 ↓
Conflict
 ↓
Punchlines
 ↓
Callback


A good bit contains:

- personal perspective
- recognizable experience
- unexpected conclusion


---

# 2. 30-Second Joke Template


## Structure


Observation
↓
Unexpected connection
↓
Punchline
↓
Tag


---

## Template Example


Topic:

Glasses


Observation:

I wear glasses.


Expectation:

They help me see.


Punchline:


Actually glasses create another problem:

I need glasses to find my glasses.


Tag:


The most advanced technology I own
depends on finding it first.


---

# 3. One-Minute Joke Expansion


Structure:


Personal statement
↓
Common experience
↓
Specific example
↓
Unexpected angle
↓
Final punch


---

Example:


Topic:

Vision


Opening:


I work in ophthalmology.


Common experience:


Everyone thinks vision is simple.


Specific example:


People say:

"Eyes are just cameras."


Unexpected angle:


A camera only records.

The eye interprets.


Final punch:


Humans invented cameras.

Then spent years trying to understand the original.

# 4. Three-Minute Bit Template


Recommended structure:


0:00-0:30
Identity + Hook
0:30-1:30
Audience connection
1:30-2:30
Professional perspective
2:30-3:00
Unexpected conclusion + callback


---

# Example Bit

## Title:

"The Eye Doctor Who Cannot See The Future"


---

## Opening


I study vision.


People think this means I can see everything clearly.


---

## Audience Connection


But everyone has vision problems.

Some people cannot see far.

Some people cannot see reality.


---

## Professional Perspective


In medicine, vision means eyesight.


In business, vision means ambition.


---

## Closing


After studying vision for years,

I learned:

The hardest thing to see

is the future.

# 5. Five-Minute Open Mic Template


Structure:


Opening identity
↓
Three observations
↓
One deeper idea
↓
Closing callback


---

Example:


## Theme:

"I Work In Eye Biotech"


Observation 1:


People are afraid of eye problems.


Observation 2:


People do not know anything about eyes.


Observation 3:


Everyone thinks they understand vision.


Deeper idea:


Seeing is not understanding.


Callback:


I study vision.

But life keeps reminding me:

I still need glasses.

# 7. Opening Templates


## Template A


"I study ______.

People think ______.

Actually ______."


Example:


"I study vision.

People think it means seeing better.

Actually, it means realizing how much humans misunderstand what they see."


---

## Template B


"I spent years learning ______.

Then I discovered ______."


Example:


"I spent years learning how eyes work.

Then I discovered humans are much worse at seeing than eyes."

---

## From Business to Personal

# 9. Closing Templates


## Callback Ending


Return to opening idea.


Example:


Opening:

I study vision.


Ending:


After all these years,

I finally understand vision.

It is not about seeing everything.

It is about knowing what you cannot see.
