# 1. Purpose


This file documents comedy structures.

The goal:

Transform:

Observation

into:

Comedy Bit


A joke is not only information.

A joke is:

A familiar truth + unexpected perspective.


---

# 2. Basic Stand-up Structure


Classic structure:


## Setup

Introduce a familiar situation.


## Assumption

Audience forms an expectation.


## Punchline

Break the expectation.


## Tag

Add additional laughs.


## Callback

Return to an earlier idea.


---

Example:


Setup:

I study eye diseases.


Audience assumption:

Medical professional.


Punchline:

People think I spend my day looking at eyes.

Actually, I spend most of my day looking at PowerPoint slides.


Tag:

The only thing I diagnose accurately is my own deadline.


---

# 3. Structure Type A

# Observation → Contradiction


Most common structure.


Formula:


Normal observation

+

Unexpected conflict

=

Comedy


---

Example:


Observation:

Humans have advanced eyesight.


Contradiction:

We use it mostly for phones.


Premise:


> Human evolution created amazing eyes.

> Civilization decided the best use was checking notifications.


---

# 4. Structure Type B

# Double Meaning


Formula:


Word with multiple meanings

+

Different interpretations


---

Example:


Word:

Vision


Medical:

Ability to see.


Business:

Future ambition.


Premise:


> Ophthalmologists restore vision.

> Entrepreneurs sell vision.

> Investors evaluate vision.


---

# 5. Structure Type C

# Expert vs Normal Person


Formula:


Expert sees complexity.

Normal person sees simplicity.


---

Example:


Doctor:

"The retina is a complex neural tissue."


Patient:

"So basically a camera."


Premise:


> Scientists spend decades understanding complexity.

> Humans summarize it in one sentence.


---

# 6. Structure Type D

# Technology vs Biology


Formula:


Human biology

vs

Artificial technology


---

Example:


Camera:

Records images.


Eye:

Processes information.


Premise:


> Humans invented cameras by copying eyes.

> Then used cameras to explain eyes.


---

# 7. Structure Type E

# Expectation Reversal


Formula:


Audience expects A.

Reality is B.


---

Example:


Setup:


I work in biotech because I want to change patients' lives.


Expectation:


Heroic mission.


Punchline:


Most days I change Excel files.


---

# 8. Structure Type F

# Status Gap


Formula:


High ambition

+

Low-level reality


---

Example:


Founder:


"We are transforming healthcare."


Reality:


Searching for meeting room availability.


---

Premise:


> Every startup begins by changing the world.

> Then spends months changing documents.


---

# 9. Structure Type G

# Analogy


Formula:


Complex concept

+

Simple comparison


---

Example:


Drug development.


Analogy:


Like raising a child.


Premise:


> Scientists create molecules like parents create children.

> They know the problems.

> They still believe in potential.


---

# 10. Structure Type H

# Personal Contradiction


Important for personal comedy.


Formula:


"I am the type of person who..."


---

Example:


> I study vision.

> My job is helping people see.

> Yet entrepreneurship is the first time I realized how blurry the future is.


---

# 11. Structure Type I

# Escalation


Formula:


Small problem

↓

Bigger problem

↓

Absurd conclusion


---

Example:


I needed glasses.


Then stronger glasses.


Then reading glasses.


At some point my glasses need glasses.


---

# 12. Structure Type J

# Scientific Translation


Especially useful for biotech.


Formula:


Scientific statement

↓

Human translation


---

Example:


Scientific:


"The mechanism requires further validation."


Human translation:


"We are not sure yet."


Investor translation:


"Please wait."


---

# 13. Building a 3-Minute Bit


Recommended structure:


## Opening

Personal identity.


Example:

"I work in ophthalmology."


---

## Section 1

Common experience.


Example:

Everyone has glasses problems.


---

## Section 2

Professional perspective.


Example:

Doctors see eyes differently.


---

## Section 3

Unexpected connection.


Example:

Vision is also business.


---

## Closing

Callback.


Example:

I started studying eyes.

I ended up learning:

The hardest thing to see is the future.


---

# 14. Callback Library


Good callbacks reuse earlier concepts.


Potential callbacks:


## Glasses

Beginning:

I wear glasses.


Ending:

My glasses help me see clearly.

Unfortunately, they cannot help me see investors clearly.


---

## Vision

Beginning:

I study vision.


Ending:

After years studying vision,

I learned humans are still bad at seeing what matters.


---

## Retina

Beginning:

The retina detects signals.


Ending:

My retina detects light.

My brain creates meaning.

My investor detects risk.


---

# 15. Rules for Eye Comedy


Avoid:


- pure medical explanation
- technical lectures
- random eye facts


Prefer:


- human experience
- contradiction
- personal vulnerability
- unexpected connection
