## 眼科医学、医生与患者沟通喜剧素材

Version: v0.1

# 1. Purpose

This file collects comedy materials from ophthalmology practice.

Topics:

- ophthalmologists
- patients
- eye examinations
- medical language
- diagnosis
- treatment expectations


Core comedy conflicts:

- expert knowledge vs patient understanding
- objective measurement vs subjective feeling
- medical reality vs emotional reaction


---

# 2. The Doctor Sees Differently


## Observation

A normal person looks at an eye and sees:

"an eye."


An ophthalmologist sees:

- cornea
- lens
- retina
- blood vessels
- optic nerve


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Different levels of perception.


---

## Premise


> The difference between patients and doctors:

> Patients see an eye.

> Doctors see a complicated biological system asking for maintenance.


---

# 3. Patient Description of Symptoms


## Observation


Patients often describe symptoms using general language:


"My eye feels strange."

"My vision is not right."

"Something is wrong inside."


---

## Conflict


Doctors need:

- location
- timing
- severity
- characteristics


Patients provide:

- emotion
- impression
- fear


---

## Premise


> Doctors ask for data.

> Patients provide a story.


---

# 4. "My Eye Is Broken"


## Observation


Patients often describe a complex disease as:

"My eye is broken."


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Complex system simplified into one sentence.


---

## Premise


> A doctor sees thousands of possible problems.

> A patient sees one problem:

> "The eye is not working."


---

# 5. Eye Examination


## Observation


Eye exams are unique.

Doctors ask:

"Can you see this?"


Patients answer:

"Yes."


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Self-reported measurement.


---

## Premise


> Eye examinations are the only medical test where the patient has to prove the machine is correct.


---

# 6. The Vision Chart


## Observation


Patients look at the vision chart.

The doctor keeps asking:

"Can you see the next line?"


---

## Conflict


The patient knows:

If they cannot see:

They have a problem.


If they can see:

The test continues.


---

## Premise


> Vision testing is a competition where the doctor keeps making the question harder until you lose.


---

# 7. Patients Searching Online


## Observation


Modern patients often search symptoms online before visiting doctors.


---

## Typical result:


Mild symptom:

Internet:

"You may have a rare disease."


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Information overload.


---

## Premise


> Before the internet:

> People worried after seeing doctors.

> Now:

> People worry before seeing doctors.


---

# 8. Medical Terminology


## Observation


Medicine uses many abbreviations:


- AMD
- DME
- GA
- VEGF
- OCT


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Professional efficiency creates communication barriers.


---

## Premise


> Medicine created abbreviations to communicate faster.

> Unfortunately, patients need a dictionary before they can worry.

# 9. Doctor Says "Not Serious"


## Observation


Doctors may say:

"It's not serious."


Patients hear:

"Everything is fine."


---

## Conflict


Doctor meaning:

Low immediate risk.


Patient meaning:

No problem exists.


---

## Premise


> Doctors and patients sometimes speak the same language.

> They just use different dictionaries.


---

# 10. Treatment Expectations


## Observation


Patients want immediate improvement.


Medicine often requires:

- time
- monitoring
- follow-up


---

## Premise


> Patients live on human time.

> Drug development lives on scientific time.

> Disease does not care about either.


---

# 11. Eye Injection Anxiety


## Observation


Some retinal treatments require intraocular injection.


---

## Conflict


The goal:

Protect vision.


The method:

Approach the eye with a needle.


---

## Premise


> Modern medicine sometimes asks people to overcome exactly what they are afraid of.


---

# 12. Ophthalmologist Personality


## Observation


Many ophthalmologists choose a field where:

- patients are usually awake
- procedures are precise
- technology is important


---

## Premise


> Ophthalmologists are people who spend their careers looking into the place where humans store their view of the world.


---

# 13. Doctor vs Patient Perspective


## Doctor:

"What is the pathology?"


## Patient:

"Will I lose my vision?"


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Different questions about the same problem.


---

## Premise


> Doctors treat diseases.

> Patients experience futures.
