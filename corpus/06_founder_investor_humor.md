# 1. Purpose

This file collects comedy materials around:

- biotech founders
- investors
- fundraising

The core comedy conflicts:

- science vs business

- long-term value vs short-term expectations


---

# 2. Founder Vision


## Observation


Almost every startup founder talks about:

"Vision."


---

## Reality


The word has different meanings.


Founder:

A future world.


Investor:

A potential return.


Employee:

A practical roadmap.


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Same word, different interpretation.


---

## Premise


> Every founder has a vision.

> The challenge is convincing everyone else that they can see it too.


---

# 3. Medical Vision vs Business Vision


## Observation


In ophthalmology:

Vision means eyesight.


In business:

Vision means ambition.


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Double meaning.


---

## Premise


> Ophthalmologists spend their lives restoring vision.

> Entrepreneurs spend their lives explaining vision.

> Both need someone else to believe them.


---

# 4. Scientist Becomes Entrepreneur


## Observation


Scientists are trained to:

- question assumptions
- analyze evidence
- avoid overconfidence


Entrepreneurs must:

- make decisions
- persuade people
- move forward despite uncertainty


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Personality transformation.


---

## Premise


> Scientists spend years learning how to say:

> "We need more data."

> Then entrepreneurship forces them to say:

> "Trust me."


---

# 5. The Scientist's Language


## Scientist:


"The mechanism suggests."


## Entrepreneur:


"This will transform healthcare."


## Investor:


"How big is the market?"


---

## Premise


> Three people can look at the same molecule.

> One sees biology.

> One sees patients.

> One sees Excel.


---

# 6. Investor Vision


## Observation


Investors also talk about vision.


---

## Conflict


Founder:

"Imagine the future."


Investor:

"Show me the path."


---

## Premise


> Founders look ten years ahead.

> Investors look for the next three years.

> Both call it vision.


---

# 7. Fundraising Reality


## Observation


Fundraising meetings often include:

- mission
- technology
- market
- team


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Grand vision meets practical questions.


---

## Premise


> The founder starts with:

> "We want to change millions of lives."

> The investor asks:

> "How much cash do you have for the next 18 months?"

# 8. The Pitch Deck


## Observation


Biotech companies spend huge effort creating presentations.


---

## Conflict


Scientific reality:

Complex.


Pitch deck:

Simple.


---

## Premise


> Drug development takes ten years.

> Explaining it to investors takes ten slides.

# 9. The First Slide Problem


## Observation


Every company wants:

"World-changing technology."


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Similarity between companies.


---

## Premise


> Every biotech company says:

> "We are first-in-class."

> The difficult part is finding the class.

# 10. Changing the World


## Observation


Founders often say:

"We want to change the world."


---

## Reality


Daily work:

- emails
- meetings
- budgets
- hiring


---

## Premise


> Startup founders begin by changing the world.

> Then spend most days changing PowerPoint slides.

# 11. Science vs Money


## Observation


Scientists ask:

"Does it work?"


Investors ask:

"Can it scale?"


---

## Premise


> Science asks:

> "Is it true?"

> Business asks:

> "Does anyone care?"


---

# 12. Long Development Timeline


## Observation


Biotech development requires patience.


---

## Three perspectives:


Patient:

"Can I get treatment?"


Scientist:

"Can we prove it?"


Investor:

"When is the milestone?"


---

## Premise


> A biotech company has three clocks.

> Disease clock.

> Science clock.

> Investment clock.


---

# 13. The Founder Blind Spot


## Observation


Founders believe deeply in their ideas.


---

## Comedy Mechanism

Confidence vs reality.


---

## Premise


> A founder's greatest strength:

> Seeing what others cannot see.

> A founder's greatest risk:

> Not seeing what others already see.

Scientists:

mechanism.


Clinical:

endpoint.


Business:

market.


---

## Premise

> Science to business.
