# Eye Wordplay Corpus

## Language-based Humor Around Eye / Vision

Version: v0.1

---

# 1. Purpose

This file collects language-based comedy materials around:

- eye
- see
- vision
- sight
- focus
- lens
- pupil

The objective is not collecting finished jokes.

The objective is identifying:

- word ambiguity
- semantic conflict
- comedy mechanisms
- possible premise generation


---

# 2. Core Word Map


## Eye

Meanings:

1. Physical organ
2. "I" in English


Comedy mechanism:

Identity confusion.


Example concept:

"Eye" is both the thing that sees and the person who sees.


Potential premise:

> The human eye is the only organ that thinks it understands everything.


---

# 3. See

Meanings:

1. Visual perception
2. Understanding


Examples:

"I see."

can mean:

- I can visually observe.
- I understand.


Comedy mechanism:

Seeing vs understanding.


Potential premise:

> Humans think seeing something means understanding it.

> History proves otherwise.


---

# 4. Vision


Meanings:

## Medical:

Ability to see.


## Business:

Future ambition.


## Personal:

Insight.


Comedy mechanism:

One word, three worlds.


Potential premise:


> Doctors spend their careers restoring vision.

> Entrepreneurs spend their careers creating vision.

> Investors spend their careers questioning whose vision is real.


---

# 5. Focus


Meanings:

## Optical:

Adjusting eyesight.


## Mental:

Concentration.


Comedy mechanism:

Physical focus vs psychological focus.


Potential premise:


> Most people don't have a vision problem.

> They have a focus problem.


---

# 6. Lens


Meanings:

## Optical:

A device that changes light.


## Conceptual:

A way of seeing the world.


Comedy mechanism:

Perspective.


Potential premise:


> Everyone looks at the same world through different lenses.

> The problem is nobody checks their prescription.


---

# 7. Sight


Meanings:

Physical seeing.

Awareness.


Potential premise:


> Losing eyesight is frightening.

> But losing insight can be more expensive.


---

# 8. Blindness


Meanings:

Physical blindness.

Cognitive blindness.


Comedy mechanism:

Literal vs metaphorical.


Potential premise:


> Humans are afraid of blindness.

> But most mistakes happen when people are certain they can see clearly.


---

# 9. Eye-related Idioms


## Keep an eye on

Meaning:

Monitor.


Comedy direction:


> Humans invented surveillance.

> Then gave it the friendly name:

> "keeping an eye."


---

## In the blink of an eye

Meaning:

Very quickly.


Potential premise:


> The fastest thing humans know is a blink.

> Except when waiting for clinical trial results.


---

## See eye to eye

Meaning:

Agree.


Potential premise:


> Humans only say "see eye to eye" when they already agree.

> Nobody says it during an argument.


---

## More than meets the eye

Meaning:

Hidden complexity.


Potential premise:


> The human eye itself is more than meets the eye.

> It took billions of years to create something we use to watch videos of cats.
